INTERPRETATION
The crystallographic QZN binding basin was recovered within modes 1-7
in 60% of independent confirmatory runs.

Successful native-like poses had RMSD values from 1.5096 to 1.6279 A.
The four failed runs produced near-site poses with RMSD values from
2.6089 to 3.0520 A and centroid displacements from 2.2171 to 2.5031 A.

The dominant mode-1 alternative basin remained highly reproducible.
In most runs, mode 1 had approximately 10.5 A RMSD and approximately
9.1 A centroid displacement relative to crystallographic QZN.

The results indicate partial but insufficiently reproducible recovery of
the crystallographic binding basin under the preregistered criterion.

DECISION
The PqsR V4 protocol is not formally validated.

In accordance with the preregistered stop rule:
- the accepted rank range will not be expanded;
- the RMSD threshold will not be relaxed;
- additional seeds will not be used to tune the acceptance result;
- V4 will not be retrospectively relabelled as successful.

For the current experimental manuscript, any PqsR docking may only be
classified as exploratory structural rationalization and must explicitly
state the redocking-validation limitation.

LasR docking remains independently validated under the V2 Vinardo protocol.

More advanced PqsR methodology, including ensemble approaches and molecular
dynamics, is reserved for the separate bioinformatics manuscript.
