STEP 9 — PqsR 4JVI exploratory docking analysis addendum

Date: 2026-07-17
Status: preregistered before production docking results were generated or inspected

SCOPE

Docking of compounds 08, 09, and 11 to PqsR/MvfR 4JVI is exploratory.
Formal PqsR redocking validation was not achieved. No result from this
analysis may be described as validated docking, demonstrated target
engagement, binding affinity, or proof of biological mechanism.

DOCKING PARAMETERS

The frozen docking parameters remain unchanged:
- receptor: PqsR_4JVI
- crystallographic reference ligand: QZN, site A
- scoring function: Vinardo
- box centre: -33.489, 56.865, 9.100 A
- box dimensions: 30 x 30 x 30 A
- exhaustiveness: 64
- num_modes: 20
- energy_range: 5 kcal/mol
- CPUs: 8
- independent seeds: 5 per ligand
- all generated modes retained

PRIMARY PREREGISTERED ANALYSIS

A pose is classified as orthosteric only when its unweighted heavy-atom
centroid is no more than 5.0 A from the crystallographic QZN heavy-atom
centroid.

Primary consensus clustering:
- direct fixed-frame heavy-atom RMSD
- no structural superposition
- RMSD threshold: 2.0 A
- consensus occupancy: at least 3 of 5 independent seeds

The primary result remains the principal spatial classification and will
not be relaxed retrospectively.

SECONDARY EXTENDED EXPLORATORY ANALYSIS

Because PqsR docking is explicitly hypothesis-generating, a secondary
sensitivity analysis will also examine pocket-proximal poses.

A pose qualifies for extended exploratory review when at least one of the
following conditions is met:

1. ligand heavy-atom centroid distance from QZN is greater than 5.0 A but
   no more than 8.0 A; or

2. the pose directly engages the crystallographic QZN pocket:
   - pocket residues are defined objectively as receptor residues having
     at least one heavy atom within 5.0 A of crystallographic QZN;
   - at least one ligand heavy atom lies within 4.0 A of a heavy atom from
     this predefined pocket-residue set;
   - minimum ligand-to-QZN heavy-atom distance is no more than 5.0 A.

Extended exploratory clustering:
- direct fixed-frame heavy-atom RMSD
- no structural superposition
- RMSD threshold: 2.5 A
- recurrent exploratory motif: poses represented in at least 2 of 5 seeds
- a pose represented in only 1 of 5 seeds is classified as isolated and
  non-reproducible

SELECTION RULES FOR AN EXTENDED EXPLORATORY MOTIF

If one or more recurrent pocket-proximal clusters are present:

1. select the cluster represented in the largest number of seeds;
2. if tied, select the cluster with the more favourable median Vinardo score;
3. if still tied, select the cluster with the lower median within-cluster RMSD;
4. select the lowest-scoring pose within that cluster as the representative.

PLIP may be applied only to:
- a primary orthosteric consensus pose; or
- a recurrent extended exploratory pocket-proximal motif represented in
  at least 2 of 5 seeds.

Any PLIP result from the second category must be labelled:
"exploratory pocket-proximal interaction profile".

REPORTING RESTRICTIONS

Permitted wording:
- exploratory docking
- orthosteric-like pose
- pocket-proximal pose
- recurrent exploratory motif
- structural hypothesis
- interaction pattern consistent with possible pocket engagement

Prohibited wording:
- validated PqsR binding
- confirmed PqsR inhibitor
- experimentally determined affinity
- proven direct target
- causal mechanism established by docking

Vinardo scores must not be compared quantitatively between LasR and PqsR.
The wet-lab phenotype establishes biological activity but does not identify
PqsR as the direct molecular target.

FUTURE WORK

Formal receptor-specific validation, explicit-solvent molecular dynamics,
binding-pose stability, water-mediated contacts, and free-energy analysis
are outside the current manuscript and are reserved for the separate
