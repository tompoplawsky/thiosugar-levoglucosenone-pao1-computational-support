STEP 9 — PQSR-SPECIFIC CONFIRMATORY REDOCKING VALIDATION PROTOCOL V4
Date: 2026-07-16
Status: preregistered before inspection of any V4 docking result

SCOPE
This protocol applies exclusively to PqsR/MvfR, PDB 4JVI.
LasR/OHN has already passed its independent V2 Vinardo validation protocol.

DEVELOPMENT HISTORY
Protocols V1-V3 constituted the method-development dataset.

V1, default Vina scoring:
- a native-like QZN pose was recovered at mode 8;
- heavy-atom RMSD: 1.7193 A;
- mode 1 was spatially incorrect.

V2, Vinardo scoring:
- a native-like QZN pose was recovered at mode 5;
- heavy-atom RMSD: 1.5289 A;
- mode 1 was spatially incorrect.

V3, five-seed Vinardo top-5 validation:
- preregistered top-5 criterion passed in 1/5 runs;
- native-like QZN poses were nevertheless recovered reproducibly at ranks 5-7;
- ranks of the best native-like poses: 5, 6, 7, 7, and 6;
- four of five runs recovered a pose with heavy-atom RMSD <= 2.0 A in modes 1-7.

The V1-V3 outputs are retained and remain classified according to their
original preregistered criteria. They are not retrospectively relabelled.

SYMMETRY AUDIT
RDKit heavy-atom graph analysis identified one automorphism for QZN:
the identity mapping only.

Therefore:
- explicit INDEX MAP RMSD equals symmetry-aware heavy-atom RMSD;
- no chemically equivalent atom permutation changes the RMSD result.

CONFIRMATORY DESIGN
V4 uses only new random seeds not used during V1-V3 development.

The only acceptance-rule change relative to V3 is expansion of the inspected
candidate set from modes 1-5 to modes 1-7.

No receptor, ligand, box, scoring-function, exhaustiveness, number of modes,
or energy-range parameter is changed.

RUN-LEVEL SUCCESS CRITERION
A run is successful when at least one of modes 1-7 satisfies both:
- symmetry-aware heavy-atom RMSD <= 2.0 A relative to crystallographic QZN;
- centroid displacement from crystallographic QZN <= 5.0 A.

RMSD RULES
- hydrogen atoms excluded;
- explicit correspondence from the retained Meeko INDEX MAP;
- no nearest-neighbour coordinate matching;
- QZN has no non-trivial heavy-atom automorphism;
- direct mapped RMSD is therefore the final symmetry-aware RMSD.

PROTOCOL-LEVEL ACCEPTANCE CRITERION
The PqsR V4 protocol is validated when at least 8 of 10 independent runs
satisfy the run-level success criterion.

SECONDARY REPORTED METRICS
For every run report:
- rank of the best native-like pose in modes 1-7;
- rank of the best native-like pose in modes 1-20;
- heavy-atom RMSD;
- centroid displacement;
- Vinardo score;
- score difference relative to mode 1;
- mode 1 RMSD and centroid displacement;
- number of heavy atoms used.

Also report:
- total successful runs;
- distribution and median rank of native-like poses;
- RMSD range;
- score-difference range;
- reproducibility of the alternative mode-1 basin.

INTERPRETATION
Successful V4 validation would demonstrate reproducible recovery of the
crystallographic QZN binding basin among the seven highest-ranked Vinardo
candidate poses.

It would not demonstrate that:
- the crystallographic pose is ranked first;
- Vinardo scores are experimental binding free energies;
- small score differences establish relative ligand affinity.

PRODUCTION CONSEQUENCE
PqsR production docking may begin only if V4 passes.

A separate production protocol must then be frozen before inspection of
compound 8, 9, or 11 docking results. It must retain:
- the same receptor;
- the same search box;
- Vinardo scoring;
- exhaustiveness 64;
- 20 modes;
- multiple independent seeds;
- explicit orthosteric spatial filtering;
- consensus analysis across seeds.

STOP RULE
If V4 fails, no additional expansion of the accepted rank range, RMSD
threshold, centroid threshold, or further seed-driven protocol tuning will
be adopted in STEP 9.

In that case:
- PqsR docking will not be classified as formally validated;
- any PqsR docking results may only be presented as exploratory structural
  rationalization with the validation limitation stated explicitly.

PROVENANCE
Retain all V1-V4 protocols, configurations, raw outputs, logs, timing files,
RMSD tables, QC decisions, software-version records, and SHA-256 checksums.
No previous result may be overwritten.
